Sumo Wrestling

Sumo wrestling is more than just a sport; it’s a vibrant tradition deeply embedded in Japanese culture. Known for its grand rituals and powerful matches, sumo offers a fascinating glimpse into Japan's rich heritage.

What Is Sumo Wrestling?

Sumo wrestling is a traditional Japanese sport where two wrestlers, known as rikishi, compete in a circular ring to force each other out or to make any part of the opponent’s body, other than the soles of their feet, touch the ground. With origins dating back over a thousand years, sumo combines physical strength with ritualistic elements and is deeply intertwined with Shinto religion.

Rules of the Game

  • Ring-Out: A wrestler wins by forcing their opponent out of the ring (dohyo).
  • Touchdown: The match is won if any part of the opponent’s body, other than the soles of their feet, touches the ground.
  • Illegal Moves: Biting, hair pulling, and striking below the belt are prohibited.
  • Starting Position: Wrestlers begin from a crouched position and must try to break the opponent’s balance and push them out.
  • Victory: Matches are typically very short, lasting just a few seconds, as the goal is to use strength and technique to gain a quick advantage.

Where to Watch

To fully experience the excitement of sumo wrestling, catch a live match or visit a sumo stable (beya). Major sumo tournaments, or basho, are held in Tokyo, Osaka, Nagoya, and Fukuoka throughout the year. For an authentic experience, consider attending a tournament, or if you’re in Tokyo, you can watch sumo practice sessions at various stables.
