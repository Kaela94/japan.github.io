Shrines

Japanese shrines are more than mere religious sites — they are architectural marvels and cultural landmarks that offer deep insights into Japan’s spiritual and historical heritage.

What Are Japanese Shrines?

Japanese shrines, or jinja, are places of worship dedicated to Shinto deities. They are characterized by their distinctive torii gates, sacred grounds, and various ceremonial practices. Unlike temples, which are associated with Buddhism, shrines are central to Shinto beliefs and rituals.

Shrine Etiquette

Observing proper etiquette at Japanese shrines is crucial for showing respect and ensuring a meaningful visit. Key practices include:

  • Purification: Wash your hands and mouth at the purification fountain (temizuya) before entering the shrine grounds.
  • Offering Ritual: Toss a coin into the offering box, bow twice, clap your hands twice, and make your prayer or wish.
  • Respectful Behavior: Remain quiet and respectful during your visit, avoiding disruptive behavior.
  • Photography: Follow any rules regarding photography, as some areas may be off-limits.

Understanding and following these customs enhances your experience and shows respect for local traditions.

Must-See Shrines in Japan

Japan is home to numerous stunning shrines, each with its own unique history and charm. Here are some of the most notable ones to include in your visit:

  1. Fushimi Inari Shrine - Kyoto

    Known for its iconic thousands of red torii gates, Fushimi Inari is a major Shinto shrine dedicated to Inari, the deity of rice and agriculture.


  2. Meiji Shrine - Tokyo

    Nestled in a lush forested area, Meiji Shrine is dedicated to Emperor Meiji and Empress Shoken and is a tranquil escape from the bustling city.


  3. Itsukushima Shrine - Miyajima Island

    Famous for its "floating" torii gate, Itsukushima Shrine is a UNESCO World Heritage site and a beautiful example of Shinto architecture.


  4. Asakusa Shrine - Tokyo

    Located near the famous Senso-ji Temple, Asakusa Shrine is known for its vibrant festivals and traditional architecture. It plays a central role in the lively Asakusa district.


  5. Namba Yasaka Jinja - Osaka

    Namba Yasaka Jinja is renowned for its striking lion head-shaped stage, which is believed to bring good fortune and protect against evil spirits. The shrine’s unique architecture and vibrant festivals make it a must-visit in Osaka.
