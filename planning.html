Planning Your Japan Itinerary

Japan offers so many things to see and do that it is essential to plan your trip to help ensure you don't miss out on anything! Follow these steps to make a plan that keeps you organized and on budget so you can get the most out of your trip.

How to Start Planning Your Trip

  1. Set Your Budget: Determine how much you’re willing to spend. Japan can be pricey, but with careful planning, you can enjoy the country on a variety of budgets. Consider accommodation, food, transportation, and activities.


  2. Decide When to Go: Japan has four distinct seasons, each offering unique experiences. Spring is famous for cherry blossoms, summer for festivals, autumn for vibrant foliage, and winter for skiing and hot springs.


  3. Research Destinations: Japan is more than just Tokyo and Kyoto. Explore the countryside, visit smaller towns, and discover hidden gems. Decide if you want to focus on cultural experiences, nature, or a mix of both.


  4. Book Accommodation: Hotels, ryokan (traditional inns), and Airbnb options are plentiful. Booking in advance ensures better rates and availability, especially during peak seasons.


  5. Plan Transportation: Japan’s public transportation is efficient and reliable. Consider purchasing a Japan Rail Pass if you plan on traveling long distances. Local trains, buses, and taxis are also convenient for getting around.
